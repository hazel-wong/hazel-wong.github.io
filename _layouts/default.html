About Me

Hi! I am a fresh graduate of the University of Michigan and one of the co-founders of HardBoiled Studios, a student-led game studio that is currently developing their debut game, Yolked.

I am passionate about machine learning. In my senior year, I conducted research on machine learning prediction biases with Serena Xiong, a fellow undergraduate researcher, and Dr. Sindhu Kutty, a professor of Machine Learning. Our research was presented at the virtual Grace Hopper Conference and won third place in the ACM Student Research Competition.

On the side, I also love to develop games. After taking a video game development course in university, two students and I took our final project, Yolked, and decided to start HardBoiled Studios and bring it all the way to the finish line. We have partnered with a publisher, Hitcents, to turn Yolked into a published intellectual property by the end of 2021.

Devblog

Project Randomizer is a research project that explores procedural generation in video games.

It has been estimated that the video game industry will be a $159.3 billion dollar industry in 2020, with estimations that it will exceed $200 billion by 2023 (Newzoo 2020) and $300 billion by 2025 (Forbes 2020). In addition, the world currently has 2.7 billion gamers, which is more than a third of the world’s population (Newzoo 2020). As the industry grows exponentially and is changing rapidly, it is important that we continue finding ways to improve and polish video games.

Video game developers may often feel stuck when creating levels during development as there may be limited time, cost and memory. Procedural generation is a way for data in video games, such as levels, characters and the environment, to be created algorithmically. This method could have multiple advantages, including creating dynamic content, saving memory usage, saving development time and saving money (Packt 2016). This project will attempt to explore that concept by creating an algorithm that will procedurally generate levels for a novel puzzle game, allowing more dynamic content to be created for people to enjoy while saving time, cost and memory usage.
